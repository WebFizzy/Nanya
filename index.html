    My Research Experience

    I have a strong background in Biostatistics with undergraduate research experience, my focus was centered on assessing the impact of Aframomum Melegueta on glucose levels in male albino rats, a study with implications for diabetes management. The rats were induced with Aloxan, and their glucose levels were meticulously monitored over a 24-hour period. The key takeaways from this research were the significant difference in mean responses observed between preparations and errors. In my research journey as an MSc student, I delved into the realm of mixture experiments, specifically comparing two distinctive types of designs: the simplex lattice design and the simplex centroid design. This comparative analysis was conducted considering different numbers of components, namely, n = 3 and 4. Within this investigation, the A, D, and G alphabetic optimality criteria were used as guiding principles to assess the efficiency of these designs.
